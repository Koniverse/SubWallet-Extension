## Goal

Everything between *a dApp asks to connect* and *the connection is trusted and stable*: the connect
popup, the connected/rejected/lost states, reconnection after a network or internet drop, and
enabling the chain a dApp needs before it can be used.

## Status

> **✅ done — all 56 rows below are settled**: 51 delivered, 5 closed without shipping. **This is the
> largest story in the epic.** It carries **no FR** — the connectors themselves are FR-94 … FR-101;
> this story is the connection *state machine* they all share.
>
> **`version_shipped: 1.3.55` is a representative anchor, not the whole set** — the most recent
> constituent with a provable release, the same convention
> [US-9.13](US-9.13-nft-media-and-ipfs-gateway-pipeline.md) uses. The table is the full record.
>
> The open half is [US-10.17](US-10.17-open-dapp-connection-and-signing-improvements.md)
> ([AGENTS.md](../../../AGENTS.md) rule 9).

## Scope

Folded in from the former one-issue-per-story maintenance ledger on 2026-07-24. It exists because
**EPIC-10's FR map is one requirement per connector and half the maintenance is not per-connector**:
losing a connection, showing the wrong connection status, or failing to enable a network happens the
same way whether the dApp arrived over EVM, Substrate or WalletConnect. Filing each of these under
whichever connector happened to be in use would be a fiction.

> **"Connection lost" and "internet lost" are the recurring shape.** #225, #700, #702 (a reconnect
> loop), #802 (reconnecting too many times on mobile), #1792, #1868, #1959 — a wallet whose
> connection depends on a chain RPC and a relay it does not control spends a great many rows
> deciding what to do when either goes away.
>
> **Enabling the network is a precondition nobody asked for until it broke.** #2899 and #2924
> (extension and WebApp) — *"automatically enable the network when connecting via the Substrate
> provider"* — a dApp on a chain the user has disabled cannot connect, and the honest fix is to turn
> the chain on rather than fail.
>
> **#4245 is Bitcoin dApp connect, shipped**, while its round 2 (#4482) is open in
> [US-10.5](US-10.5-bitcoin-dapp-connection-psbt.md). Connecting a Bitcoin dApp is a lifecycle
> concern; signing a PSBT for it is the FR that has not shipped.
>
> **#4375 is the connection failing for a reason outside the wallet**: *"SubWallet flagged by Avast,
> blocks connection to Polkadot.js"*. An antivirus false-positive is still a connection-lifecycle
> defect from the user's seat.
