## Goal

Make a Ledger device usable on **one more network** — the recurring per-chain work that follows
every Ledger integration. Each row is a chain (or a chain-specific Ledger app) brought onto the
signing surface [US-16.1](US-16.1-ledger-hardware-wallet-signing.md) already established.

## Status

> **✅ done — all 19 rows below are settled**: 15 delivered, 4 closed without shipping. It carries
> **no FR** — FR-144 is owned by [US-16.1](US-16.1-ledger-hardware-wallet-signing.md); this story
> holds the chain-by-chain coverage that followed it.
>
> **`version_shipped: 1.3.12` is a representative anchor, not the whole set** — the most recent
> constituent with a provable release, the same convention
> [US-9.13](US-9.13-nft-media-and-ipfs-gateway-pipeline.md) uses. The table is the full record.

## Scope

Folded in from the former one-issue-per-story maintenance ledger on 2026-07-23. Split from
[US-16.7](US-16.7-generic-ledger-app-migration-and-metadata.md) on the axis that actually divides
the work: **this story adds a chain, US-16.7 changes how every chain is reached.** The first is
bounded per-network work; the second is a platform migration whose cost is paid once and then
maintained forever.

> **Vara is the whole argument for the Generic app in two rows.** #2283 — *"Support Ledger for the
> Vara network"* — closed `NOT_PLANNED`; two years later #3835 shipped Vara in 1.3.9 **through the
> Generic app**, not through a Vara-specific one. The per-chain route was abandoned; the platform
> route delivered ([US-16.7](US-16.7-generic-ledger-app-migration-and-metadata.md)).
>
> **#1962 and #2357 are the same request, answered in half.** #1962 asked for Centrifuge, Darwinia
> **and** Energy Web Chain together and closed `NOT_PLANNED`; #2357 shipped Energy Web Chain's EWT
> token alone. Two of the three chains never arrived.
>
> **#4225 is `deprecated` because the board says `Cancel`.** The tracker closed it `COMPLETED`, so
> the generated ledger read it as shipped — but a closed issue carries two statuses and the board is
> the specific one ([AGENTS.md](../../../AGENTS.md) rule 12). #4357 is the same shape and the ledger
> already had it right.
>
> **#4980's board still reads `Ready to build`** while the tracker has it closed `COMPLETED`. Here
> the board is the stale one — it records the column nobody moved, not a cancellation, so the
> tracker wins. The two directions are distinguishable: `Cancel` is a decision, `Ready to build` on
> a closed issue is an omission.
