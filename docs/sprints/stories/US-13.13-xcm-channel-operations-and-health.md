## Goal

Keep the **channel list honest while nobody is looking**: check the routes on a schedule, hide the
ones that are broken, push the corrected list without shipping an extension, and be able to author
the pair files by hand when the automation is wrong.

## Status

> **✅ done — all 10 rows below are settled**, 3 with a release and 7 delivered with no line naming
> them. It carries **no FR**: this is the operational half of
> [NFR-15](../../PRD.md#non-functional-requirements) and
> [AD-09](../../ARCHITECTURE.md#architecture-decisions) — every XCM route is individually toggleable
> at runtime — exercised rather than built.
>
> **`version_shipped: 1.3.55` is a representative anchor, not the whole set** — the most recent
> constituent with a provable release. The table is the full record.

## Scope

Folded in from the former one-issue-per-story maintenance ledger on 2026-07-23. Separated from
[US-13.12](US-13.12-xcm-route-and-channel-coverage.md) because the two answer different questions:
that story asks *does this route exist*, this one asks *does it still work today*. The second has no
finish line — it is why the online chain-list toggle exists at all.

> **"Recheck XCM Weekly" is a tracker issue, and that is the whole point.** #1598 has no scope, no
> release and no finish — it is a **standing chore filed as a ticket**, which is what happens when a
> capability's correctness depends on two hundred chains that upgrade on their own schedules. #2171
> and #2579 are the same instinct with narrower titles.
>
> **Three rows hide a route rather than fix it**: #1440 (Moonbeam, 1.0.6), #2717 (blocked channels
> not updating, 1.1.43) and #4444 (1.3.55). The runtime toggle
> ([AD-09](../../ARCHITECTURE.md#architecture-decisions),
> [NFR-15](../../PRD.md#non-functional-requirements)) exists so that a partner chain going bad costs
> a config push, not a release — and #2717 is the release you have to ship when *the toggle itself*
> stops applying.
>
> **#4444's CHANGELOG bullet is narrower than its issue.** The 1.3.55 line reads *"Remove MYTH
> (PAH → Ethereum)"*, but the tracker title is *"Hide some XCM channels"* — the issue was widened
> after the note was written, and MYTH is the instance the release notes happened to name.
>
> **#4208 shipped and its swap twin did not.** The same tooling for *swap* pairs
> ([#4209](https://github.com/Koniverse/SubWallet-Extension/issues/4209)) is still `Follow Up` in
> [US-11.16](US-11.16-swap-flow-improvements.md).
