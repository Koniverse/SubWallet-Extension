## Goal

Show **how a proxy account allocated the proxied account's balance** in the earning surface —
the stake history of the proxy, read from the proxied account's point of view
([#4947](https://github.com/Koniverse/SubWallet-Extension/issues/4947)). Open, not started.

## Scope

This is a **consolidated maintenance story**: it groups 1 tracker issue into one capability with a
clear boundary, replacing the former one-issue-per-story ledger. It materializes **no FR** —
FR-148 is fully owned by [US-17.1](US-17.1-proxy-types-and-authority-management.md) (the authority
model) and [US-17.2](US-17.2-proxy-signing-sign-selector-and-proxied-by-display.md) (signing and
provenance display), both `done` in 1.3.72. Full issue→story traceability is the table below and
the [consolidation note](../../notes/2026-07-22-epic-17-consolidation.md). **Not yet delivered** —
`assignee` / `commit` / `sprint` / `version_shipped` stay empty until it ships.

**Why it is its own story and not a row in US-17.2.** US-17.2 is `done`; #4947 is open and asks
for something it never delivered. Folding unfinished work into a shipped story is how a `done`
story comes to carry an unticked AC — the split the owner directed after the NFT consolidation:
completed work stays in the completed story, remaining work becomes its own improvement story.

It also **straddles two epics**: the proxy relationship is EPIC-17's, but the surface it must
appear on is the earning/staking portfolio ([EPIC-12](../epics/EPIC-12.md)). Filed here because
the *question* it answers — "what did this proxy do with my balance" — is a proxy-provenance
question; the earning screen is where the answer is rendered.
