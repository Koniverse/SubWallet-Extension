## Goal

OpenGov Phase 2 deepens governance participation: a user can browse referenda
**by governance track**, and **delegate** their voting power (with a conviction
multiplier) on a chosen track to another account — and later undelegate. This
lets a holder who cannot follow every referendum still have their stake count, and
lets power users organize participation per policy area rather than one global
vote.

## Background

OpenGov splits referenda into **tracks** (Root, Treasury tiers, Whitelisted
Caller, etc.), each with its own approval/support curves and lock behaviour.
Phase 1 ([US-15.1](US-15.1-opengov-referenda-and-conviction-voting.md)) shipped
referenda browse + direct conviction voting; Phase 2 adds the two remaining
OpenGov primitives users ask for: **per-track organization** of the referenda
surface and **delegation** (`convictionVoting.delegate` / `undelegate`), where a
user assigns their voting power on a track to a delegate at a conviction
multiplier — which itself creates a conviction lock.

Track and delegation data is aggregated through the Services SDK
([AD-24](../../ARCHITECTURE.md#architecture-decisions)); the delegate/undelegate
extrinsics are constructed and signed in the background and surfaced via the typed
`pri()` bus ([AD-03](../../ARCHITECTURE.md#architecture-decisions)); the resulting
delegation lock is read over the lightweight WsProvider path
([AD-07](../../ARCHITECTURE.md#architecture-decisions)) and rendered/released
through the same locked-token machinery as a direct vote
([US-15.2](US-15.2-locked-token-detail-and-unlock-flow.md)). This story reuses the
referenda subject fixture and signing path established by US-15.1.

Materializes [FR-142](../../PRD.md#functional-requirements). This story is **📋 planned / forward** — the
Phase-2 expansion of the shipped OpenGov surface, not yet started. Tracked by
[#4679](https://github.com/Koniverse/SubWallet-Extension/issues/4679) —
[OpenGov][Extension] Phase 2.

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] **AC-1** — **Given** an OpenGov chain with multiple governance tracks,
  **When** the user opens the referenda surface, **Then** referenda can be browsed
  and filtered by track, with each track's metadata shown.
- [ ] **AC-2** — **Given** a track and a sufficient free balance, **When** the
  user delegates their voting power on that track to another account at a chosen
  conviction, **Then** the `delegate` extrinsic is built and signed in the
  background and the delegated amount becomes conviction-locked.
- [ ] **AC-3** — **Given** an existing delegation on a track, **When** the user
  undelegates, **Then** the `undelegate` extrinsic is submitted and the track
  delegation is removed (the lock then follows the unlock flow of US-15.2).
- [ ] **AC-4** — **Given** the user attempts to delegate more than their available
  free balance, **When** they confirm, **Then** the flow blocks with an
  insufficient-balance error and no extrinsic is submitted.
- [ ] **AC-5** — **Given** an invalid delegate target (e.g. delegating a track to
  the user's own account or a malformed address), **When** they confirm, **Then**
  the flow blocks with a validation error rather than submitting.

## Dev notes

### Architecture constraints

- [AD-24](../../ARCHITECTURE.md#architecture-decisions) — track and delegation data is aggregated through the Services SDK backend; this surface consumes it.
- [AD-07](../../ARCHITECTURE.md#architecture-decisions) — the delegation-lock/free-balance read rides the lightweight WsProvider path; the full ApiPromise is instantiated only to construct the delegate/undelegate extrinsic.
- [AD-03](../../ARCHITECTURE.md#architecture-decisions) — delegate/undelegate extrinsics are signed in the background and surfaced via `pri()`; no key material in the governance UI.
- This story does NOT introduce new AD entries.

### Cross-story dependencies

- Builds on [US-15.1](US-15.1-opengov-referenda-and-conviction-voting.md) — extends the referenda surface, the referenda subject fixture, and the background signing path.
- Builds on [US-15.2](US-15.2-locked-token-detail-and-unlock-flow.md) — the delegation lock is rendered and released through the same locked-token machinery.
- Consumes the free/transferable figure from [EPIC-7](../epics/EPIC-7.md)'s balance model for the insufficient-balance guard.

### What we explicitly did NOT do

- No on-device per-chain track/delegation indexer — data comes through the Services SDK (AD-24).
- No bespoke delegation-lock display — reuses the US-15.2 locked-token detail + unlock flow rather than a parallel surface.
