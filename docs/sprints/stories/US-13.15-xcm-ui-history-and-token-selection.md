## Goal

What the user sees around a cross-chain transfer: the screen they pick a token on, the warning
before they commit, and — afterwards — a history entry that shows the transfer actually happened.

## Status

> **✅ done — all 9 rows below are settled**: 7 delivered, 2 closed without shipping. It carries
> **no FR** — FR-126 is owned by [US-13.1](US-13.1-xcm-parachain-transfers.md).
>
> **`version_shipped: 1.2.14` is a representative anchor, not the whole set** — the most recent
> constituent with a provable release. The table is the full record.

> **#402 and #418 have the same title, word for word.** One closed `NOT_PLANNED`, the other
> shipped — a duplicate pair sixteen issue numbers apart, and the ledger carried both as separate
> stories without noticing. The same shape as #4161/#4162 in
> [US-7.2](US-7.2-transferable-vs-locked-balance-calculation.md).
>
> **XCM history needed a third party.** #586 (0.5.7) found history entries missing after an XCM
> transfer, and #660 answered it by integrating the **Polkaholic API** — a cross-chain transfer
> produces events on two chains and neither one alone is the record. #1499 is the opposite error:
> showing an entry twice when the sender and recipient are the same account.
>
> **#528 — "add a warning" — is the honest version of a feature that could not yet be verified.**
> It predates the dry-run work in
> [US-13.14](US-13.14-xcm-fee-dry-run-and-validation.md) by years: when the wallet could not tell
> whether a transfer would arrive, it told the user so.
