## Goal

Answer, before the user signs, the two questions a cross-chain transfer cannot answer by itself:
**how much will this actually cost**, and **will it arrive**. Delivery fees, destination-chain fees,
existential deposits, max-transferable, recipient validation — and finally simulating the extrinsic
instead of predicting it.

> **1.3.31 is the release where the wallet stopped predicting and started asking.** Five of these
> rows land in it together — #3615 (integrate the dry-run API), #4134 (dry-run XCM), #4133 (estimate
> the delivery fee), #4233 (recipient validation) and #4387 (*"making sure teleports don't fail"*).
> Before it the extension computed what an XCM *should* cost; after it, it simulates the extrinsic
> on-chain and reads the answer.
>
> **#2792 is the same problem, four releases early, closed `NOT_PLANNED`.** *"Handle XCM delivery
> fee"* was the right question in 1.1.x and had no good answer until the dry-run API existed. Its
> successor is #4133, and the gap between them is why fee bugs (#1218, #1550) stayed open in the
> meantime.
>
> **"Max transferable" is asked three times and answered differently each time**: #3617 for the
> native token (1.2.30), #3876 for EVM accounts (1.3.8), #4676 against both a minimum and a maximum.
> Its bridge form is #3911 in [US-13.3](US-13.3-avail-bridge.md). The recurring difficulty is that
> the sender's chain cannot see what the destination will charge.
