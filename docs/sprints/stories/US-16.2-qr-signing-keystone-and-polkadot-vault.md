## Goal

A user can sign fully offline with an air-gapped QR device — Keystone or Polkadot
Vault / Parity Signer — by scanning the payload to the device and the signature
back, so that the signing key never touches a connected machine.

## Background

The QR transport family. The wallet displays the signing payload as a QR code, the
air-gapped device scans it, signs, and shows the signature QR to scan back — no
cable, no network on the device. It is the strongest form of the offline keyring
boundary ([AD-04](../../ARCHITECTURE.md#architecture-decisions),
[NFR-14](../../PRD.md#non-functional-requirements)). Keystone and Polkadot Vault share the same
display→scan→display round trip and differ only in device-specific QR framing, so
they are one story. Separated from Ledger
([US-16.1](US-16.1-ledger-hardware-wallet-signing.md)) because the QR transport is
a different integration from USB.

Materializes [FR-147](../../PRD.md#functional-requirements) and [FR-148](../../PRD.md#functional-requirements). **Retroactive** —
already shipped.

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] **AC-1** — **Given** a Keystone or Polkadot Vault account, **When** the user
  signs a transaction, **Then** the wallet shows the payload QR, accepts the
  scanned-back signature, and submits — fully offline on the device side.
- [ ] **AC-2** — **Given** a large payload that exceeds one QR frame, **When**
  signing, **Then** the multi-frame (animated) QR is produced and the device can
  reassemble it.
- [ ] **AC-3** — **Given** an invalid or mismatched signature scan, **When** the
  user scans it back, **Then** the wallet rejects it with a clear error and
  submits nothing.
