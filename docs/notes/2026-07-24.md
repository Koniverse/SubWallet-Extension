## A. EPIC-28 (Maintenance — Transactions) merged into EPIC-8

**200 ledger stories dissolved; 212 rows across 15 stories,** seven of them new. The transactions
ledger held one story per tracker issue. It is deleted; its issues are now incremental-work rows on
the capability they belong to.

Arithmetic: 200 ledger issues − 1 umbrella (#4371) + 3 the ledger had merged into other rows (#118,
#143, #3067) + 9 named in EPIC-8's prose and held by no table + 1 recovered from the swap fold
(#4434) = **212 rows**. Four of those rows then left the epic — three to
[US-7.2](../sprints/stories/US-7.2-transferable-vs-locked-balance-calculation.md) and one to
[US-16.11](../sprints/stories/US-16.11-qr-device-signing-defects.md).

### Sending costs fifty-nine rows; receiving costs three

[US-8.1](../sprints/stories/US-8.1-send-native-and-fungible-tokens.md) took **59 of the 212** and
roughly two thirds of them name a chain or a token: Acala, Karura, Kintsugi, Astar, Bifrost,
Pioneer, Equilibrium, Genshiro, Moonbeam, Polygon, Polimec, Tangle, Autonomys, TON, Cardano,
Bitcoin, Bittensor.
[US-8.2](../sprints/stories/US-8.2-receive-qr-and-copyable-address.md) (receive) took **three**.

That asymmetry is the clearest measure in the docs of where cross-chain cost actually lands. An
address does not change when a chain is added; a transfer extrinsic does, every time, and the answer
is never inherited. The one receive row a chain *did* create — #4486, *"error validating the receive
address when transferring MYTH"* — only appears **inside a transfer**, and needed a hot fix line as
well as a release line.

### The 1.0.2 rewrite is bigger than the UI fold could see

[US-6.7](../sprints/stories/US-6.7-the-1-0-2-ui-upgrade-programme.md), written on 2026-07-23, holds
60 rows recovered from the UI ledger and says the programme "is held together by a title prefix
rather than by a parent". That was true and it was also incomplete: **11 more `Upgrade UI - ` rows
were sitting in the transactions ledger** — the transaction screens, the receive screen, the
transaction-type components, the background handler. They are now
[US-8.17](../sprints/stories/US-8.17-transaction-screens-in-the-1-0-2-rewrite.md).

**The real size of the rewrite is 71 rows, not 60**, and a programme with no parent issue was
invisible to the generator **twice, in two different areas**. There is no reason to think the
remaining five ledgers hold none.

Two rows in that story are the duplicate the naming convention invites: **#1051 *"Transactions
Screen"*** (opened 2023-02-27, closed `NOT_PLANNED`) is **#1024 *"Upgrade UI - Screen
Transactions"*** (opened twenty days earlier, shipped 1.0.2) filed again without the prefix. #1063
and #971 are the same pair for the background half.

### "Transfer max" is the most re-opened phrase in the epic

Seven attempts across five years at one number, all in
[US-8.15](../sprints/stories/US-8.15-transfer-max-and-available-balance.md): #1220 reviewed it,
#1439 improved it, #1458 validated it for local tokens, #2793 handled it being computed too slowly,
#2817 disabled the submit button until it arrives, #4462 found it still wrong in 1.3.58, #4821
refactored the whole available-balance path in 1.3.68. Three more (#1549, #1922, #2541) are still
open.

**#2793 and #2817 are the same insight from two directions** — the max is asynchronous and the user
can press Send before it lands. One fixed the submitted amount; the other stopped the press. Only
the second is durable.

- **[US-8.16](../sprints/stories/US-8.16-evm-fee-and-gas-estimation.md)** — the wallet guesses one.

And the structural answer to both, **#2618 *"implement new fee service"***, has been `In Backlog`
since 2024. Every fee row filed since is interest on that decision. The open remainder is five more
fee issues in [US-8.12](../sprints/stories/US-8.12-fee-bigint-and-gas-estimation-hardening.md).
